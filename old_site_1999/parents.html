A day in the life of a 5west-er

(parent version)

6:00 AM: Awake, bright and early with the sun. Make bed.
6:15 AM: Write daily email to mom, dad, with report of latest academic conquests followed by a refreshing shower.
6:45 AM: Time for eggs, bacon, milk, orange juice, and toast with a side of Textbook.
7:10 AM: Brush. Floss. Shave, if applicable. Floss again.
7:15 AM: Review in daily planner today's tasks. Create a to-do list and enter it in palm-pilot.
7:30 AM: Make appearance on the hall. Read newspaper, to brush up for next year's Geography and Current Events Bowl. Exchange greetings with other early risers, trading insightful comments into the recent fluctuations observed in the NYSE. Review notes to prepare for first lecture.
9:00 AM: Begin fun-filled day of lecture!
12:00 PM: Lunch with Physics professor.
12:55 PM: Use traveling toothbrush, floss.
1:00 PM: Ready for a few more hours of lecture!
4:45 PM:Check mail. Notice that your latest patent was approved. Got a licensing fee check for previous patents. Send money home to mom.
5:00 PM: Arrive back home, tackle problem set due in two weeks.
6:00 PM:Break for a light, yet nutritious dinner of chicken breast, rice, potato, milk.
6:38 PM:Brush teeth, floss.
9:00 PM: Finished with problem set! Just in time to get an hour in on next month's problem set!
9:22 PM: A hall mate drops by to say hi and ask for help on problem 2, which is provided most cheerfully!
10:00 PM:Time for bed... A brief exchange with hall mates on way to bathroom for a final tooth brushing/flossing.
10:10 PM:Get a spooky feeling. Get up again, floss teeth. Make bed again. Crawl back into bed. Feel much better and fall asleep.
